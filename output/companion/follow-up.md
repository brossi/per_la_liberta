# Follow-up — open research leads

Outstanding items worth chasing on **Cesare Crespi** (the book's author) and related threads. None of these is needed to *correct* the companion — his identity and dates are settled (see below). They are **enrichment**: sources that would deepen the [Crespi biography in `personae.md`](personae.md) or add color. Each entry gives what it is, why it matters, where it lives, how to get it, and what it would add.

## What's already established (so this doc stands alone)

**Cesare Crespi** — born near Milan, **1857**; died San Francisco, **28 November 1948**. A radical-republican Italian-American journalist: fled to Scotland (early 1880s) with Giuseppina Alberti, via New York, settled San Francisco 1885. Founded *Il Messaggero* and *Secolo Nuovo* (first Italian anarchist paper on the US West Coast); co-founded *La Protesta Umana* (1900) with Enrico Travaglio; edited *L'Italia*, *La Colonia Svizzera*, *La Voce del Popolo*. Nine Italian-language books in San Francisco, 1906–1947, including *Per la libertà!* (1913). Second wife **Sylvia Travaglio**; children include daughter **Nina (Mrs Lucarini)**; home at 961 Vallejo St.

**Sources already in hand:** Kenyon Zimmer, *Immigrants Against the State* (2015); *San Francisco Chronicle* obituary, 28 Nov 1948, and California Death Index 1940–1997 (both via GenealogyBank/FamilySearch); Leonardo Bettini, *Bibliografia dell'anarchismo* (FICEDL ed. — correct on b. 1857, **wrong** on d. 1940); James J. Periconi Collection (Calandra Italian American Institute); Francesco Durante, *Italoamericana*; Crocker-Langley San Francisco directories.

**Search keys for any record system:** `Cesare` / `Caesar` Crespi · b. ~1857, Italy (near Milan) · d. San Francisco, 28 Nov 1948 · wife Sylvia · 961 Vallejo St · occupation journalist/editor.

## 1. Lucarini's biographical article — the prize *(high value)*

> **Umberto Lucarini, "Cesare Crespi," *La Parola del Popolo*, no. 37 (Dec 1958–Jan 1959), pp. 286–287.**

The fullest known life of Crespi, written by his **son-in-law** (Nina Crespi's husband — the same Lucarini named in the 1948 obituary). It is the underlying source later accounts (Zimmer included) ultimately draw on.

- **Where it lives:** inside the ~600-page **50th-anniversary special** of *La Parola del Popolo* (*cinquantesimo*, 1908–1958), which *was* issue no. 37.
- **Why it's hard:** University of Illinois–Chicago digitized the whole surrounding run on Internet Archive — issues **32–36 (1958)** and **38–43 (1959)** — and **issue 37 is the single gap** (confirmed by direct query; no monograph copy on IA either). And as a **1958** publication it's still under US copyright, so no library offers it open full-view.
- **How to get it (in order of reliability):**
  1. **IHRC, University of Minnesota** (Elmer L. Andersen Library) — principal US holder. Request a fair-use **scan of pp. 286–287**. Cite **OCLC 2243512**; specify the *bound 1908–1958 cinquantenario special (issue 37)*, **not** the 1921–24 microfilm. *(Draft email below.)*
  2. **HathiTrust** record `000499614` with an institutional login — may allow a member to search/view the closed 1958 volume.
  3. **ILL / "Scan & Deliver"** from any WorldCat holder of the cinquantenario.
  4. **Google Books** bound volume (issues 32–37, id `PilbAAAAMAAJ`) — snippet view only, but a browser may surface lines around "Crespi."
- **What it would add:** a continuous, family-authored life story — likely his exact Milan birthplace, emigration details, the full SF journalism career, and personal character.

**Ready-to-send IHRC scan request (draft):**

## 2. Durante, *Italoamericana* — headnote *(quick win)*

Francesco Durante's anthology *Italoamericana: The Literature of the Great Migration, 1880–1943* (Fordham UP, 2014; Italian orig. Mondadori, 2 vols.) almost certainly carries a **Crespi headnote that cites Lucarini and summarizes him** — a fast secondary route that also confirms the citation chain.

- **Where:** the Hofstra/Pugliese intros PDF, JSTOR (`j.ctt13x0432`), Fordham UP, or the Mondadori Italian edition. *(These Cloudflare-block automated tools but open normally in a browser.)*
- **What it would add:** a scholarly précis of the Lucarini article and likely an explicit birth/death line.

## 3. *San Francisco Chronicle* obituary — full text, 28 Nov 1948

We have the GenealogyBank *index entry* (the source of the death date and family names); the **actual obituary text/image** is the next step.

- **Where:** GenealogyBank (you already have access there) — open the underlying *San Francisco Chronicle*, 28 Nov 1948 document; FamilySearch record ark `61903/1:1:Q57M-98HB`.
- **What it would add:** the paper's own account — age, career summary, surviving family, and likely funeral/burial details, in print.

## 4. California death certificate / full death-index record

Now that the date is fixed (28 Nov 1948, San Francisco), the **death certificate** is orderable.

- **Where:** California Dept. of Public Health — Vital Records, or the **San Francisco Assessor-Recorder / County Clerk** (county death records post-1906 survive).
- **What it would add:** cause of death, **exact birthplace (Italian comune?)**, parents' names, occupation, the informant (likely Sylvia), and burial place — the single richest civil record.

## 5. Casellario Politico Centrale dossier (Rome) *(server currently down)*

Zimmer cites a Fascist political-police file: **Casellario Politico Centrale, busta 1530, Archivio Centrale dello Stato (ACS), Rome.**

- **Where:** the CPC database at `http://dati.acs.beniculturali.it/CPC/`; also the SIUSA portal (`https://siusa.archivi.beniculturali.it/`) and the ACS site (`https://acs.cultura.gov.it/`). All were unreachable from this environment (possibly an IP/geo block rather than a true outage) — worth trying from a normal browser, with an Italian VPN exit if needed. The ACS OPAC `https://search.acs.beniculturali.it/` is up but indexes only inventory-level descriptions, not name-level fascicoli.
- **What it would add:** the Italian state's surveillance view — birthplace comune, emigration record, catalogued political activity, and often a closing date that would independently corroborate the 1948 death.

## 6. Burial — Italian Cemetery, Colma *(optional)*

The secular **Cimitero Italiano**, Colma (run by the Italian Mutual Benevolent Society) — the plausible resting place for an anticlerical.

- **Where:** FamilySearch collection **1922526** (index cards + daily log; log begins 1934, so a 1948 burial is in range), free login; or direct lookup at **[italiancemetery.com](https://www.italiancemetery.com/)** / **(650) 755-1511**.
- **What it would add:** grave location, burial date, age, and often birthplace.
