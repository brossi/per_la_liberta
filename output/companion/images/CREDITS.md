# Image Credits & Provenance

This is the full provenance record for the public-domain images that illustrate the [Reader's Companion](../index.md). It is the picture analogue of the companion's sourcing discipline: every image is a verified public-domain work, and every entry below records the *observed* rights statement — read off the source page at the time of access, not assumed.

## How these were chosen

- **Public domain by age.** A faithful photographic reproduction of a public-domain two-dimensional work (a pre-1900 engraving, painting, or photograph) is itself public domain regardless of which library digitized it. Where an image existed in several places, the copy with the cleanest, least ambiguous rights statement was preferred; the holding institution is credited in every case as a courtesy and for traceability. Anything whose public-domain status could not be cleanly established was rejected — including several modern CC-licensed photographs of monuments and surviving sites.
- **Period-preferred.** Contemporary 19th-century engravings, lithographs, paintings, and photographs were preferred over modern images. A few period *photographs* postdate the events they accompany (e.g. the Belluno panorama of 1918, the La Roquette photograph of c. 1876) but remain the best public-domain views of the place.
- **Honest captions.** Captions distinguish an actual likeness or site from a *contemporary depiction* of an event (a representation, not a photograph of the moment). Event scenes — the Five Days, the siege of Rome, the attentat, Aspromonte — are paintings and drawings, and are labelled as such in-line.
- **Stable citations.** The "Source page" column gives the canonical Wikimedia Commons *File:* page (or other repository item page), which persists. The volatile direct-download links used to fetch the files are deliberately not recorded here.

All images accessed **2026-06-08**. Oversized originals were downscaled to a 1600-px long edge; no other alteration was made.

## Deliberately left unillustrated

In keeping with the companion's anti-fabrication discipline, several entries carry no image because no authentic, cleanly public-domain one could be found — and a misrepresented or wrongly-licensed substitute would be worse than none:

- **Pietro Fortunato Calvi** — the only Commons images are modern, CC-licensed photographs of monuments and busts, not a period likeness.
- **The execution of Orsini and Pieri (13 March 1858)** — no clean-PD contemporary depiction of the scene was located.
- **Cesare Crespi** (the author) — no verified photograph of the right Cesare Crespi (the San Francisco journalist–publisher) exists in the open repositories; "Cesare Crespi" hits on Commons are all other men.
- **Signora Elisa, Don Bastiano Barozzi, Baron di Torocfalda**, and the named **ships** (*Radetzky*, *La Durance*, *Abeille*, *Amazzone*, *John Romilly*, *Virginia*) — no authentic likeness or vessel image exists.
