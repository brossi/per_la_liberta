Choosing a body face

Each block below sets a real passage from Chapter One. The chapter number and title are Bodoni Moda (display) — that stays, per the pairing decision — and the running body text changes per candidate. Every face here is variable with an optical-size axis and font-optical-sizing:auto, so the phone frame (390 px) renders the genuine small-size cut, not a shrunk display cut. The desktop column is capped at the research measure (~66ch / 34em). Compare the phone frames first — that's where Bodoni fails and the decision is really made.

Bodoni Moda — current baseline (body + display)

What we ship today. A Didone: gorgeous at title size, but the hairline serifs and extreme thick/thin contrast thin out and shimmer at reading size — worst on the phone.

Chapter One

A Morning in Los Angeles

Los Angeles, a splendid summer morning. I had arrived there but a few hours since, on a train that had put me in mind of those which, from every part of France, converge upon the shrine at Lourdes.

For the metropolis of Southern California, situated near the ocean, between the desert and the orange groves, is a great center of attraction for all those who have the misfortune to live beyond the Rockies — where, in summer, the sun prostrates men and herds alike, like withered grass.

Perchè la metropoli della California meridionale, situata presso l'oceano, tra il deserto e gli aranceti, è un gran centro di attrazione per quanti hanno la disgrazia di vivere al di là delle Rocciose — dove il sole prostra, come erbe appassite, uomini e mandrie.

«Che cosa desiderate?» — domandò severamente. Tre quarti di secolo; un'incessante sequela di lotte, di pericoli, di patimenti…

New directions — this round

Source Serif is out. These three open registers the first four never touched: a warm humanist/Garalde book hand, the actual 1900s "Modern" a 1913 edition was set in, and an editorial transitional with a text optical. Tradeoff to weigh: EB Garamond and Old Standard have no optical-size axis, so they won't auto-thicken on the phone the way the variable faces do — judge whether their period character is worth it at 390 px.

EB Garamond

The canonical humanist book face — Claude Garamond's 16th-c. types, warm and calligraphic. Maximum literary prestige; a wholly different texture from anything above. Watch for spindliness at phone size (no opsz, light Garalde) — Crimson Pro is the sturdier swap if it reads thin.

Old Standard TT

A revival of the classicist "Modern" serifs used in late-19th / early-20th-c. editions — the most historically honest face here for a 1913 book; it looks like the period. Trades technical modernity for authenticity: static weights, no opsz, more dated spacing.
